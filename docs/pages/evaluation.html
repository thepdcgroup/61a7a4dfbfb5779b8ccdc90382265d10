Evaluation
An Earth-like planet is also known as a ____ planet.


terrestrial
The most important factor for a planet to be considered Earth-like is the presence of ____.


solid surface
A planet's ability to support liquid water depends largely on its ____.


habitable zone
The habitable zone is sometimes called the ____ zone.


Goldilocks
Kepler-22b is an exoplanet located in the ____ system.


Kepler
One of the most promising Earth-like exoplanets is ____.


Proxima Centauri b
Kepler-442b is notable for having a strong potential for ____.


habitability
TRAPPIST-1 has a total of ____ known planets.


seven
The TRAPPIST-1 system is located approximately ____ light-years from Earth.


40
The discovery of Proxima Centauri b was made using the ____ telescope.


ESOs HARPS
An exoplanet is a planet that orbits a ____.


star outside our solar system
A planet's atmosphere is crucial for maintaining ____.


surface temperature
Kepler-62f is considered to be in the habitable zone of its ____.


parent star
The James Webb Space Telescope is helping scientists analyze exoplanet ____.


atmospheres
The transit method is used to detect exoplanets by measuring ____.


dips in star brightness
The Kepler Space Telescope was responsible for discovering thousands of ____.


exoplanets
Liquid water is considered essential for ____.


life as we know it
The radial velocity method detects exoplanets by observing ____.


wobbles in a star's motion
Kepler-186f is sometimes called Earth's ____.


cousin
An exoplanet's ability to retain an atmosphere depends on its ____.


gravity
The term "habitable zone" means the region where ____.


liquid water can exist
The discovery of TRAPPIST-1 planets suggests that Earth-like planets may be ____.


common
Scientists use spectroscopy to study the ____ of exoplanets.


chemical composition
Kepler-69c is an exoplanet that is believed to be similar in size to ____.


Earth
The search for Earth-like planets is important because it helps us understand the potential for ____.


extraterrestrial life
